§ 02 · Model Performance

Nine models, one spatial winner.

Comparison of parametric, machine-learning, and spatial models across two metrics. While gradient boosting frameworks capture non-linearities well, only Geographically Weighted Regression accommodates the local heterogeneity intrinsic to maternal health geography — yielding a 35% improvement in R² over the best global ML model.

§ 03 · Spatial Autocorrelation Diagnostics

Global models leave spatial signal on the table.

Moran's I statistic quantifies residual spatial autocorrelation — the extent to which model errors cluster geographically rather than scatter randomly. A well-specified model should yield residuals with I ≈ 0. OLS residuals retain substantial spatial structure (I = 0.447, p < 0.001), indicating omitted geographic processes. GWR residuals approach spatial independence (I = 0.034, p = 0.18), validating local model specification.

On methodology

This dashboard accompanies a spatial epidemiology analysis extending Khan et al. (2023, JAMA Network Open) to county-level preterm birth risk in the United States. The analytic framework integrates three validated indices — CDC/ATSDR SVI, Surgo MVI, and USDA RUCA — within a spatial machine-learning pipeline benchmarked against Geographically Weighted Regression.

Khan SS, Petito LC, Huang X, et al. Preterm Birth and Postpartum Readmissions Associated With Adverse Pregnancy Outcomes in a Contemporary US Cohort. JAMA Netw Open. 2023;6(4):e237934.
